The Eternal City

Rome (Roma) has been Italy's capital since 1871 and serves as the nation's political, cultural, and spiritual center. With a history spanning over 2,700 years, Rome was once the heart of the mighty Roman Empire.

  • Population: ~2.8 million (city), ~4.3 million (metro)
  • Region: Lazio
  • Founded: 753 BC (traditional date)
  • Elevation: 21 meters (69 ft)

Significance

Rome is home to:

  • Vatican City - the world's smallest independent state
  • The Italian Parliament and government
  • Countless UNESCO World Heritage sites
  • Major international organizations (FAO, IFAD, WFP)

The city sits on the Tiber River and is famous for its seven hills: Aventine, Caelian, Capitoline, Esquiline, Palatine, Quirinal, and Viminal.

Landscape & Terrain

Mountains

Italy is predominantly mountainous, with two major ranges:

  • The Alps: Form Italy's northern border, including Mont Blanc (Monte Bianco) at 4,808m - Western Europe's highest peak
  • The Apennines: Run the length of the peninsula, often called "the backbone of Italy"
  • The Dolomites: UNESCO World Heritage mountain range in the northeast

Plains & Valleys

The Po Valley (Pianura Padana) in northern Italy is the largest plain, home to:

  • Italy's most fertile agricultural land
  • Major industrial centers
  • The Po River - Italy's longest river (652 km)

Other notable valleys include the Arno Valley (Tuscany) and Tiber Valley (Lazio).

Coastline & Islands

Italy's extensive coastline borders four seas:

  • Adriatic Sea: Eastern coast
  • Ionian Sea: Southern coast
  • Tyrrhenian Sea: Western coast
  • Ligurian Sea: Northwestern coast

Major islands include Sicily (largest Mediterranean island), Sardinia, Capri, Ischia, and Elba.

Volcanoes

Italy has several active and dormant volcanoes:

  • Mount Etna: Europe's most active volcano (Sicily)
  • Mount Vesuvius: Famous for destroying Pompeii in 79 AD
  • Stromboli: Continuously active volcanic island
  • Vulcano: Part of the Aeolian Islands
