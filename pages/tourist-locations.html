Top Tourist Destinations

Discover Italy's most captivating locations - from ancient cities to coastal paradises. Plan your perfect multi-day Italian adventure.

Lazio Region

Rome

The Eternal City is a living museum where ancient history meets vibrant modern life. Walk through the ruins of the Roman Empire, marvel at Renaissance masterpieces in the Vatican, and enjoy authentic Italian cuisine in charming trattorias.

From the iconic Colosseum to the Trevi Fountain, Rome offers endless discoveries that warrant extended exploration over several days.

Getting There

Rome Fiumicino Airport (FCO) serves international flights. The city center is accessible via Leonardo Express train (32 min) or taxi. High-speed trains connect Rome to Florence (1.5 hrs) and Naples (1 hr).

Tuscany Region

Florence

The birthplace of the Renaissance, Florence is an art lover's paradise. Home to Michelangelo's David, Botticelli's masterpieces, and the stunning Duomo, this city shaped Western civilization's cultural heritage.

Beyond the museums, Florence offers world-class shopping, exceptional Tuscan cuisine, and serves as the perfect base for exploring the rolling hills of Chianti wine country.

Getting There

Florence Airport (FLR) handles European flights. Alternatively, fly into Rome or Pisa and take a train. High-speed trains from Rome take just 1.5 hours. The compact city center is best explored on foot.

Veneto Region

Venice

There's nowhere else on Earth quite like Venice. This magical city built on water features winding canals, ornate palaces, and hidden piazzas that reveal themselves only to those who wander its labyrinthine streets.

Experience a gondola ride at sunset, explore St. Mark's Basilica, and lose yourself in a city where every corner is a photograph waiting to happen.

Getting There

Venice Marco Polo Airport (VCE) is connected to the city by water taxi or bus to Piazzale Roma. The Santa Lucia train station receives high-speed trains from Milan (2.5 hrs), Florence (2 hrs), and Rome (3.5 hrs).

Campania Region

Amalfi Coast

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Amalfi Coast is a stunning stretch of coastline featuring dramatic cliffs, pastel-colored villages, and crystal-clear Mediterranean waters.

Visit the charming towns of Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello. Enjoy fresh seafood, limoncello made from local lemons, and some of Italy's most breathtaking views.

Getting There

Fly into Naples International Airport (NAP), then take a ferry from Naples or Sorrento, or rent a car for the scenic coastal drive. The winding roads are best navigated by experienced drivers or via SITA bus service.

Lombardy Region

Milan

Italy's fashion and financial capital, Milan combines modern sophistication with rich cultural heritage. The magnificent Gothic Duomo, Da Vinci's Last Supper, and world-class shopping in the Quadrilatero della Moda await.

Beyond fashion, Milan offers exceptional dining, vibrant nightlife, and serves as a gateway to the stunning Italian Lakes region.

Getting There

Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP) is Italy's second-largest airport with global connections. The Malpensa Express train reaches the city center in 50 minutes. Milan Centrale station is a major rail hub connecting all of Italy.

Liguria Region

Cinque Terre

Five picturesque fishing villages cling to the rugged Ligurian coastline, connected by scenic hiking trails and a charming local train. Each village - Monterosso, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola, and Riomaggiore - has its own distinct character.

Hike between villages, swim in secluded coves, and savor fresh pesto and local white wine as the sun sets over the Mediterranean.

Getting There

The nearest major airports are Pisa (PSA) and Genoa (GOA). Take a train to La Spezia, then the Cinque Terre local train. Cars are not permitted in the villages - this is a destination best explored on foot and by rail.

Sicily Region

Sicily

Italy's largest island is a world unto itself, blending Greek temples, Norman cathedrals, and Baroque cities with stunning beaches and Europe's most active volcano, Mount Etna.

Explore the ancient ruins of Taormina, wander through Palermo's vibrant street markets, and discover a culinary tradition that rivals any on the mainland.

Getting There

Palermo (PMO) and Catania (CTA) airports receive flights from major European cities. Ferries run from Naples and other mainland ports. A rental car is recommended to explore the island's diverse attractions.

Lombardy Region

Lake Como

Nestled in the foothills of the Alps, Lake Como has enchanted visitors for centuries with its deep blue waters, elegant villas, and lush gardens. This glamorous retreat has attracted everyone from Roman aristocrats to modern celebrities.

Take a ferry between lakeside villages, explore historic Villa del Balbianello, and enjoy the refined atmosphere of Bellagio, often called the "Pearl of Lake Como."

Getting There

Milan Malpensa (MXP) is the closest major airport. Trains run from Milan Centrale to Como in about an hour. From Como, ferries and hydrofoils connect the various lakeside towns.
